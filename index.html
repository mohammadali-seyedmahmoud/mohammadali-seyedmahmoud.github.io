About me

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in the George W. Woodruff School of Mechanical Engineering at Georgia Tech, where I'm fortunate to be advised by Prof. Surya Kalidindi.

My research sits at the intersection of materials informatics, solid mechanics, and probabilistic modeling, with a focus on applying machine learning and Bayesian inference to materials characterization and damage modeling.

In my work, I use tools from machine learning, Bayesian inference, finite element simulations, surrogate modeling, and uncertainty quantification. My research is motivated by enabling rapid, data-driven materials characterization—from small-scale specimens to constitutive model calibration— while quantifying uncertainty in model predictions.
