About me

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in the George W. Woodruff School of Mechanical Engineering at Georgia Tech, where I'm fortunate to be advised by Prof. Surya Kalidindi.

My research sits at the intersection of physics-based and data-driven modeling for materials systems. I develop finite element models (ABAQUS), Python-automated simulation pipelines, and ML/surrogate models for accelerated materials design and process optimization — spanning constitutive and damage modeling (GTN ductile fracture in aluminum alloy AA6111), property-driven microstructure design, and high-throughput parameter identification.

My work integrates Bayesian inference, uncertainty quantification, generative modeling (flow matching), Gaussian Process surrogates, and experimental data (load–displacement curves, full-field DIC strain fields) for model calibration and materials knowledge extraction. I am motivated by enabling rapid, uncertainty-aware materials characterization — from small-scale specimens to constitutive model calibration — by replacing expensive simulations with learned surrogates and generative models.

Research

Sequential Bayesian Inference for Ductile Damage Modeling Acta Materialia, 2026

Developed a surrogate-accelerated Bayesian inference framework to calibrate the GTN ductile damage model for aluminum alloy AA6111. The framework simultaneously processes load–displacement curves and full-field DIC strain fields as multimodal observables, using Gaussian Process emulation to replace expensive ABAQUS finite element simulations inside the MCMC/TMCMC inference loop. The result is full posterior distributions over GTN parameters — quantifying not just best-fit values but their uncertainty and correlations — enabling uncertainty-aware damage predictions for automotive aluminum sheet.

Flow Matching for Posterior Sampling in Inverse Material Characterization Manuscript in preparation

Developing a flow matching-based generative framework for high-throughput, uncertainty-aware constitutive property identification from Small Punch Test (SPT) specimens. The framework learns a transport map from a simple prior to the posterior distribution, replacing MCMC with a single fast forward pass after training. Conditioning data integrates synchronized load–displacement curves, full-field DIC measurements, and ABAQUS FE simulations. Benchmarked against MCMC/TMCMC baselines on accuracy and computational cost.

AI-Assisted Microstructure Realization for Property-Driven Design Materials, 2023

Built an ML-guided pipeline for in-silico microstructure realization targeting thermal conductivity objectives in refractory alloys. The pipeline generates synthetic microstructures, simulates their properties, trains a surrogate model on the resulting dataset, and optimizes toward a target property — solving the inverse design problem without exhaustive physical experimentation. Directly relevant to alloy design and process optimization in the metals industry.
